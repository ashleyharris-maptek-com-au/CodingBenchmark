Implemented `main.rs` in Rust (edition 2024) to generate a non-intersecting set of tetrahedrons whose combined vertical projections (shadows onto z=0) cover the polygon’s bounding box, which in turn covers the polygon.

Approach:
- Read polygon vertices and sun direction.
- Compute polygon centroid and axis-aligned bounding box.
- Use adaptive strategy based on polygon scale/complexity:
  - Small: try 1 tetrahedron (scale <= 10) centered at centroid; otherwise grid with max scale.
  - Medium: density-based grid sizing with small alternating yaw and slight z-staggering to avoid 3D intersections.
  - Large/complex: fast coarse tiling using max scale with generous overlap.
- Output each tetrahedron as center position, quaternion, and uniform scale.
