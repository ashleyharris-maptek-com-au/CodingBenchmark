I have successfully implemented a C# Minesweeper solver. The solution includes a `Program` class for input/output and a `MinesweeperSolver` class that encapsulates the core logic. The solver uses a queue-based iterative approach with a `HashSet` to efficiently propagate deductions across the board. It identifies provably safe cells by applying two fundamental rules: if a numbered cell's mine count is fully accounted for by confirmed mines, its remaining unknown neighbors are safe; conversely, if the sum of confirmed mines and unknown neighbors equals the cell's mine count, then all unknown neighbors must be mines. This deductive strategy ensures no guessing and aims to find as many provably safe cells as possible, adapting to varying board complexities by continuously processing constraints until no further direct deductions can be made.
