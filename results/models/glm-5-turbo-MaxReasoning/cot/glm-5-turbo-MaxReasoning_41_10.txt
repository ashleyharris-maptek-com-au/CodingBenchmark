The user wants a SPIR-V assembly fragment shader that:

2. Alternates between red (0.9, 0.1, 0.1) and blue (0.1, 0.1, 0.9)
3. Applies Lambertian shading with factor `(0.4 + 0.6 * diffuse)`
4. Outputs alpha 1.0

Steps in the shader:
1. Read uv.y
2. Compute sin(uv.y * 3.14159 * 16.0)
3. If sin > 0, use red, else use blue
4. Read normal and normalize it
5. Compute light direction (lightPos.xyz - worldPos.xyz), normalize
6. Compute diffuse = max(dot(N, L), 0.0)
7. Compute factor = 0.4 + 0.6 * diffuse
8. Multiply stripe color by factor
9. Output vec4(result, 1.0)
