**Limitations of the current implementation (due to time constraints and the complexity of CSG from scratch):**
-   **Incomplete Union:** This implementation primarily classifies existing triangles. It keeps triangles from Mesh A that are outside Mesh B, and triangles from Mesh B that are outside Mesh A. It *does not* generate new faces along the intersection boundary. Therefore, the resulting mesh will likely have "holes" or be non-watertight where the two input meshes intersect. A complete CSG Union requires robust clipping and re-triangulation of intersection loops, which is a highly complex task.
-   **Vertex Deduplication:** Vertex deduplication uses a `std::map` with approximate equality checks. While functional, for extremely large-scale meshes or certain degenerate cases, more robust spatial hashing or clustering might be required.
-   **Ray-Casting Degeneracies:** Although efforts were made to handle grazing intersections in the ray-casting algorithm, full robustness against all geometric degeneracies (e.g., rays passing exactly through edges or vertices) is challenging without more sophisticated geometric predicates or perturbation techniques.
