The Nemhauser-Trotter theorem provides a way to identify vertices that must be in or must be out of the optimal vertex cover. The procedure:

1. Create bipartite graph G' = (V_L ∪ V_R, E') where for each vertex v in original graph, we create v_L in V_L and v_R in V_R. For each edge (u,v) in original graph, we add edges (u_L, v_R) and (v_L, u_R).
2. Find maximum matching M' in G'
3. Let U = unmatched vertices in V_L
4. Let Z = vertices reachable from U via alternating paths
5. Let K_L = V_L \ Z, K_R = V_R ∩ Z
6. Minimum vertex cover of G' is K_L ∪ K_R

Then in the original graph:
- v is in V1 (must be in optimal cover) if v_L ∈ K and v_R ∈ K
- v is in V0 (must not be in optimal cover) if v_L ∉ K and v_R ∉ K
- v is in V½ (undecided) otherwise

For V½, the minimum vertex cover of the subgraph induced by V½ has size ≥ |V½|/2.
