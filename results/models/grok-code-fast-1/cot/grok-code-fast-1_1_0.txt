## Understanding Task Scope  
- The task is to solve the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) in C++, handling city counts from 10 to 10 million or more.  
- The algorithm needs to adapt dynamically based on the input size for scalability.  

## Defining Input and Output  
- Input consists of N (number of cities) followed by N lines of x and y coordinates as floating-point numbers.  
- Output format is not fully specified, suggesting further clarification is needed for implementation.

## Analyzing Scalability  
- For small cases (10-40 cities), brute force is feasible, but scaling beyond 15 cities becomes impractical due to factorial growth.  
- For larger cases (up to 10 million cities), exact solutions are impossible within 300 seconds, requiring fast approximations like Nearest Neighbor.  

## Identifying Algorithm Choices  
- Use Nearest Neighbor for medium to large inputs (100-10M), starting at city 0 and always choosing the closest unvisited city.
