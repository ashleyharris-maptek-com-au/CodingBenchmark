**Writing minimal SPIR-V fragment shader assembly**

I'm creating SPIR-V assembly for a fragment shader that takes a normalized normal vector as input, normalizes it, maps it to a color by scaling and offsetting to [0,1], and outputs it with alpha 1. I'll define necessary capabilities, types, variables, constants, and use `OpExtInst` from GLSL.std.450 for normalization. The shader will only declare and use the normal input and output color, avoiding unused constructs like UBOs. I'll build the instructions step-by-step to handle vector operations and final color construction carefully with `OpCompositeExtract` and `OpCompositeConstruct`.
