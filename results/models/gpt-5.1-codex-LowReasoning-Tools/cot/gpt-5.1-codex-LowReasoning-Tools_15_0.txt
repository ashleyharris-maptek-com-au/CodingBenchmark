**Designing adaptive tetrahedron shadow coverage**

I’m working out a Rust program to cover a polygon’s shadow using tetrahedrons based on the sun’s direction. Since ensuring perfect coverage without intersections is tough, I’m focusing on approximating coverage using scaled and rotated tetrahedrons arranged in a grid and separated along the sun vector to avoid overlaps, aligning the tetra’s local -Z axis with the sun direction, and placing them above the plane to keep shadows consistent and non-intersecting. This approach balances complexity and feasibility while respecting scale limits and covering the polygon as fully as possible.**Planning tetrahedron placement and rotation**

I’m refining the algorithm to place tetrahedrons along the sun direction to avoid intersections while keeping them above the ground plane, using rotation matrices to align each tetrahedron’s local -Z axis with the sun vector. The plan includes generating sample points inside the polygon, creating candidate tetrahedron positions with different scales on a grid, computing their shadow projections precisely, and selecting coverage greedily until the polygon’s shadow is fully covered, all while carefully managing spacing along the sun vector for no overlaps and respecting scale limits.**Outlining polygon sampling and candidate generation**
