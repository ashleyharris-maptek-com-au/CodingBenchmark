For an orbital TSP, we need to compute the optimal path that visits all stations. The "cost" between two points is typically the delta-V required to transfer between orbits.

**Key physics for orbital transfers:**
1. **Hohmann transfer** is the most common (minimum energy) for coplanar orbits
2. The delta-V for a Hohmann transfer from orbit 1 to orbit 2:
   - v1 = sqrt(μ/r1)
   - v2 = sqrt(μ/r2)
   - Transfer orbit perigee velocity: v_perigee = sqrt(2μ*r2/(r1*(r1+r2)))
   - Transfer orbit apogee velocity: v_apogee = sqrt(2μ*r1/(r2*(r1+r2)))
   - Δv1 = v_perigee - v1
   - Δv2 = v2 - v_apogee

For non-coplanar orbits, we need to consider plane changes. The total ΔV includes both magnitude change and direction change.

For 6-10 stations: Use 2-opt or 3-opt heuristic
For 15-20 stations: Use more advanced heuristic like simulated annealing or ant colony

            last = best;
