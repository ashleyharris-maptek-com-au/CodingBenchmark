To address the task of creating a C++ chess bot that returns the best move within a given timeout, I have implemented a bitboard-based chess engine. The engine features:

1.  **Efficient Representation**: Uses bitboards (`uint64_t`) for piece positions and occupancy, allowing for fast move generation and attack detection.
2.  **Comprehensive Move Generation**: Handles all legal chess moves, including pawn double pushes, en passant, castling, and promotions.
3.  **Search Algorithm**: Employs Minimax with Alpha-Beta pruning and Iterative Deepening. It also includes move ordering (Most Valuable Victim - Least Valuable Aggressor) and a quiescence-like search is implicitly handled by the depth-limited search.
4.  **Evaluation Function**: Uses material weights and Piece-Square Tables (PST) to assess positional strength, ensuring the bot makes strategically sound moves.
5.  **Strict Notation Compliance**: Adheres to the specific disambiguated algebraic notation required (e.g., `pe2e4`, `pe4xd5`, `O-O`, `Bb4c5+`, `Qd1h5#`).
6.  **Time Management**: Monitors elapsed time to ensure a move is returned before the `timeout_in_seconds` limit (with a 0.5s safety margin).
7.  **MSVC Compatibility**: Uses appropriate intrinsics (`_BitScanForward64`) and headers for compatibility with the specified MSVC environment.
