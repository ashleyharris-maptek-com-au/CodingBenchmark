**Key design decisions and how they handle each scale:**

| Scale | Matching Strategy | Dijkstra Strategy | Why it works |
|-------|------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Trivial** (≤26 odd) | Exact DP over subsets O(n·2^n) | Sequential Dial's | DP handles up to 26 odd vertices exactly |
| **Medium** (27-200 odd) | Greedy matching O(k²log k) | Sequential Dial's | Greedy gives reasonable approximation quickly |
| **Large** (200-1000 odd) | Greedy matching | Sequential Dial's | Bucket queue keeps Dijkstra fast |
| **Extreme** (1000-5000 odd) | Greedy matching | Parallel Dijkstra | Multi-core parallelism for shortest paths |
| **Epic** (5000-50000 odd) | Greedy matching | Parallel Dijkstra | Scales linearly with cores |

**Critical optimizations:**
1. **Dial's bucket queue** (O(V+E+W)) instead of binary heap — dramatically faster for integer weights
2. **In-place edge deletion** via linked-list adjacency with `aHead`/`aNxt` — O(1) per deletion
3. **BitArray for edge visited flags** — cache-efficient, 1 bit per edge
4. **Parallel Dijkstra** with `Parallel.For` — fully utilizes the 64-core CPU
5. **Pre-allocated bucket arrays** resized on demand — avoids per-node allocations
6. **No graph copy for augmentation** — appends to existing adjacency lists
