Let me think about the Lambertian diffuse lighting:
- N = normalize(normal) - the normalized surface normal
- L = normalize(ubo.lightPos.xyz - worldPos) - the light direction vector
- d = max(dot(N, L), 0) - the diffuse intensity (clamped to 0)
- The final color should be grayscale based on this intensity with alpha 1.0

Looking at the inputs:
- worldPos: world-space position
- normal: world-space normal (may need normalization)
- uv: texture coordinates
- tangent: tangent vector
- color: per-vertex color

The UBO contains:
- model, view, projection matrices
- lightPos: (5.0, 5.0, 5.0, 1.0)
- cameraPos: (0.0, 0.0, 3.0, 1.0)
- params: (0.0, 0.5, 0.0, 0.0)

For Lambertian diffuse lighting, I need to:
1. Normalize the normal vector
2. Calculate the light direction vector (from surface to light)
3. Calculate the dot product between normal and light direction
4. Clamp the result to 0 (no negative values)
5. Use this as the grayscale intensity
6. Set alpha to 1.0
