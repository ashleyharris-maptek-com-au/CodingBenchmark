Let me recall Stoer-Wagner algorithm.

Stoer-Wagner finds the min-cut by repeatedly finding min-cuts in contracted graphs.

It works by maintaining a sequence of min-cuts.

The algorithm is:

While the graph has more than 1 vertex:

Find a min-cut (A,B) with |A|<=|B| by computing for each vertex the sum of weights to the current "active" set or something.

It uses a phase where we contract until 2 vertices left, and the cut is the min.

Specifically:

 - Initialize the graph with N vertices.

  - While number of supernodes >1:

    - Perform a phase: start with all vertices in S = empty, pick arbitrary v, add to S.

      - Repeatedly add the vertex outside S with maximum weight to S (sum of edge weights to S).

      - Use priority queue to keep track of the cut weights.

    - When all added, the last added vertex's cut weight is the cut value for that phase, and the cut is between the last vertex and the rest.

    - Keep track of the minimum cut value over all phases, and the corresponding cut.

    - Then, contract the two vertices of the min cut found (the last two in the order), merge them into one supernode, update all edges to it by summing.
