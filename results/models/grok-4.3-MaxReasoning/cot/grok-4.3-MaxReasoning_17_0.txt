A better way: use a data structure for free space.

For example, maintain a list of free rectangular boxes (3 3D free rects), initially one big WxHxD.

For each new box, try to fit it into one of the free rects by choosing orientation that fits, place it at corner (0,0,0) of the free rect, then split the free rect into up to 3 new free 3D rects (like in 2D skyline).

This is the guillotine packing or recursive subdivision.

This is efficient, O(log) per placement if use priority queue or sorted list.
