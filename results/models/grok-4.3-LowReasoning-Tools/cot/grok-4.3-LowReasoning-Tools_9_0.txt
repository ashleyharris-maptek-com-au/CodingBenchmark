The task is to solve the 1D Cutting Stock Problem in C#. The goal is to cut pieces from stock lengths with minimal waste, but here it's about minimizing the number of stock pieces used.
The problem is the 1D Cutting Stock Problem. I need to cut smaller pieces from stock pieces of length stock_length, assigning cuts to stocks without exceeding the length, minimizing the number of stocks used.

Input: First line has N (number of cuts?) and stock_length? Wait, example:

Input:

3  100

30 40 50

So first line: N stock_length where N=3, number of cuts.

Second line: the N cut lengths.

Output:

2

0 1

2

Meaning 2 stocks, first stock gets cuts 0 and 1 (30+40=70 <= 100), second gets 2 (50).

All cuts must be assigned, each to exactly one stock, and for each stock, sum of assigned cuts <= stock_length.

Minimize number of stocks? The output is the number, and the assignment, but probably any minimal is ok, but since NP-hard, heuristic.
