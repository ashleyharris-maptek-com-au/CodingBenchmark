The task is to write a GLSL compute shader for 3D boid flocking simulation following the given interface.
First, I need to recall Reynolds' boid rules:

1. **Separation**: Steer to avoid crowding local flockmates. Sum vectors pointing away from neighbors within a certain distance.

2. **Alignment**: Steer towards the average velocity of neighbors within a certain distance.

3. **Cohesion**: Steer towards the center of mass of neighbors within a certain distance.

Also, boundary conditions.

Parameters given:

- sepRadius: 2.0, weight:1.5

- alignRadius:5.0, weight:1.0

- cohRadius:8.0, weight:1.0

- max 5.0

- boundSize:50.0, boundForce:0.5

For implementation:

In loop, for each j != i:

diff = pos - otherPos

dist = length(diff)

if dist ==0 continue

For separation: if dist < sepRadius, add normalize(diff) * weight or accumulate and normalize later.

Common way: accumulate sum of unit vectors away, then average.

For alignment: accumulate sum of otherVel, average.

For coh: accumulate sum of otherPos, average.

Then multiply by weights.

Then add to accel.
