### Proposed Strategy

1.  **Robust PID Control**: I will implement a `PID` class with anti-windup and output clamping. Four separate control loops will be used:
    *   **Pitch**: To track the glideslope or target vertical speed.
    *   **Roll**: To track the localizer and maintain wings level.
    *   **Yaw**: To align with the runway during crosswinds and compensate for asymmetric engine thrust.
    *   **Throttle**: To maintain the target approach speed (`target_vref_kt`).

2.  **Sensor Fusion and Fault Tolerance**: To handle redundant and potentially failing sensors (3x Glideslope, 3x Localizer, 3x IAS, etc.), I will use a **median filter**. This will automatically ignore outliers (biased sensors) and utilize the `_valid` flags to filter out failed sensors.

3.  **Landing Phase Management**:
    *   **Approach Phase (>50ft Radio Alt)**: Track ILS signals, deploy landing gear, and set flaps to 40°.
    *   **Flare Phase (<50ft Radio Alt)**: Reduce thrust to idle and pitch up slightly to target a gentle touchdown vertical speed (approx. -150 fpm).
    *   **Rollout Phase (On Ground)**: Deploy spoilers, apply full reverse thrust, and engage max autobrake while maintaining the centerline using the rudder.

4.  **Go-Around Logic**: If the aircraft becomes unstable on short final (e.g., glideslope or localizer deviation exceeds 2 dots below 500ft), the autopilot will automatically trigger a Go-Around: applying full TOGA thrust, pitching up to 15°, and climbing to 1500ft to reset the attempt.

5.  **Failure Compensation**:
    *   **Engine Failure**: The throttle PID will naturally increase thrust on the remaining engine, and the yaw PID will compensate for the resulting asymmetric torque using the rudder.
    *   **Jammed Controls**: The integral term in the PIDs will help compensate for partial control jams or offsets.
