I have designed and implemented an adaptive C++ TSP solver that handles varying scales of $N$ cities by employing different strategies:

1.  **Small $N$ ($N \le 10$):** Employs a brute-force approach using `std::next_permutation` to find the exact optimal tour, ensuring high-quality results for trivial cases.
2.  **Large $N$ ($N > 10$):** Utilizes a greedy Nearest Neighbor construction heuristic accelerated by a uniform grid spatial partitioning data structure. This reduces the search complexity from $O(N^2)$ to near $O(N)$ for finding the next city, making it performant enough to handle up to 10,000,000 cities within the 300-second constraint.
