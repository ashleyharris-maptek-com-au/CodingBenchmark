**Designing SPIR-V fragment shader for polka dot pattern with lighting**

I'm assembling a detailed plan to create a SPIR-V fragment shader that renders a 6x6 polka dot grid by using UV coordinates and applies Lambertian lighting based on normalized normals and a light position uniform. I'll define all needed types, constants, uniform structures—including matrices and vectors—and craft the shader instructions to handle fract operations, distance checks, diffuse shading computations, color selection, and final output assembly with alpha. There’s lots to consider here, from access chains for uniform blocks to careful vector-scalar handling for constructing the output color. I’m excited to build this step-by-step!
