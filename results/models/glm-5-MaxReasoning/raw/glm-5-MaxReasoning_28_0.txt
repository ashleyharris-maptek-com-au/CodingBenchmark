Looking at this problem, I need to solve Minimum Vertex Cover for graphs up to 2M vertices and 15M edges. Since this is NP-hard, I'll use a sophisticated approximation approach combining:

1. **Kernelization** - Reduce the problem by handling degree-0 and degree-1 vertices
2. **Maximal Matching** - Gives a guaranteed 2-approximation in O(E) time
3. **Post-processing** - Remove redundant vertices (those whose neighbors are all already in cover)

The key insight is that for a degree-1 vertex, its neighbor must be in any optimal cover. For the matching-based approach, selecting both endpoints of edges in a maximal matching guarantees all edges are covered with at most twice the optimal size.
