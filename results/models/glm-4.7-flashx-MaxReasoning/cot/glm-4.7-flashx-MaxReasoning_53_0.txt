            *   Ahead of station ($y > 0$): We are too fast. To match speed and position, we fire *Prograde* (thrust in $+y$), raising the orbit to match the station's speed/energy (or slowing down if we were on a lower orbit).
        *   $z$ (Cross-track): Thrusting in this direction raises/lowers inclination. To cancel drift, we need the station to be in our plane. Usually, we need to be in the same plane.
    *   **Regimes:**
        *   Far (>200m): Full orbital mechanics. Use Hohmann-like transfers. If behind, burn $-y$. If ahead, burn $+y$.
