1.  **Understand the Goal:** The user wants a SPIR-V binary for a fragment shader encoded as a hex string.
    *   **Input:** 5 vec3 positions (worldPos, normal), 1 vec2 UV, 1 vec3 tangent, 1 vec3 color.
    *   **UBO:** Matrices and lighting info.
    *   **Output:** vec4 color, alpha=1.0.
    *   **Logic:** UV Checkerboard (8x8, val 0.9/0.2) + Lambertian shading.
    *   **Constraints:** Raw binary, little-endian, valid SPIR-V header, valid opcodes.

2.  **Determine Shader Logic:**
    *   *Inputs:* `worldPos` (loc 0), `normal` (loc 1), `uv` (loc 2), `color` (loc 4).
    *   *UBO:* Structured buffer or block.
    *   *Checkerboard:*
        *   Calculate `freq = 8`.
        *   `int2 iuv = floor(uv * freq)`.
        *   `bool isEven = (iuv.x + iuv.y) % 2 == 0`.
        *   `vec3 baseColor = isEven ? vec3(0.9) : vec3(0.2);`
    *   *Lighting:*
        *   Need position, normal, view dir, light dir.
        *   `lightDir = normalize(lightPos - worldPos)`
        *   `viewDir = normalize(cameraPos - worldPos)`
        *   `halfDir = normalize(lightDir + viewDir)` (Blinn-Phong) or just Lambert `dot(normal, lightDir)`. The prompt says "Lambertian shading". Standard Lambertian is `dot(N, L)`.
        *   `diffuse = max(dot(normal, lightDir), 0.0)`.
    *   *Composition:* `outColor = baseColor * diffuse * inputColor`.
