        $dp[mask][v] = \min_u dp[mask][u] + dist[u][v]$ (Steiner merge).
        
        If we iterate over $u \in \text{mask}$ for every $v$, complexity is $O(3^K N \cdot K)$. If $K$ is small relative to $N$, it's roughly $3^K N$.
        Wait, the $N^2$ term usually comes from a misunderstanding of the loop bounds.
        Let's trace:
        For each mask ($2^K$), for each $v$ ($N$).
        Inside, for each $t \in \text{mask}$ (average $K/2$).
        Total ops $\approx 2^K \times N \times (2^K) \times K \approx N K 3^K$.

        BUT, we only need to do this for states that are finite.
        And we can iterate masks increasing, and update states.

        We iterate $t$ in mask.
        This is $3^K N$.
