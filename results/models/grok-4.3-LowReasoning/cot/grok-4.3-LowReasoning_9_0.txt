The task is to solve the 1D Cutting Stock Problem in C#. The goal is to cut pieces from stock lengths with minimal waste, but specifically to minimize the number of stocks used.
The problem is the 1D Cutting Stock Problem, which is essentially the bin packing problem where bins are the stocks of length L, and items are the cut lengths.

I need to assign cuts to stocks such that each stock's total length <= L, and minimize the number of stocks used. But actually, the output is the number of stocks, and for each, the list of cut indices assigned to it.

It doesn't specify to minimize, but from context, yes, minimal number.

For efficiency with millions of cuts, need a good heuristic that runs fast.

Since time is 5 min, and 64 cores, perhaps use a 1D bin packing heuristic that is parallelizable.

A standard fast heuristic is First Fit Decreasing (FFD): sort items descending, then place each in the first bin that fits, else new bin.

But as I thought, naive impl slow.

To make it fast, I can use an array of bins, but still search.
