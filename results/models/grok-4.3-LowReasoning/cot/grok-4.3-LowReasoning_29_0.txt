The task is to write a C# program for the Maximum Clique problem, which is NP-hard. It needs to handle graphs from 20 vertices to 1 million, sparse to dense, and work efficiently across all.
The problem requires finding the maximum clique in an undirected graph, and it must be exact: no larger clique exists. But for graphs with 1 million vertices, that's impossible unless the graph is special or I use a heuristic that works well in practice.

The hints say: "You may wish to drop to a huer (heuristic) if time is running out for partial score." So probably for large graphs, heuristic is acceptable for partial credit, but the success criteria say "No larger clique exists", so perhaps tests have small n or graphs where max clique is easy.
