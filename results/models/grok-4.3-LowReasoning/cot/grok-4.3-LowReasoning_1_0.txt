The task is to write a C++ solver for the Traveling Salesman Problem that handles from 10 to 10 million cities. It must output a valid tour starting from 0.
The problem is to solve TSP for up to 10 million cities in C++, outputting a permutation starting from 0.

Since it's coordinates, I assume Euclidean distance.

For large N, exact is impossible, so heuristic.

It must be correct: valid permutation, but not necessarily optimal.

Scalable, adaptive.

For small 10-40: brute or exact.

But brute for 40 is impossible (40! huge).

Brute force with backtrack and pruning for small.

But better: use standard TSP heuristics.
