The task is to implement a CSG Union operation in C++ for meshes of any complexity, from small to millions of vertices. It must be efficient, correct, and produce a watertight manifold mesh.
The task is to write a C++ program that reads two meshes A and B from stdin, computes their union, and outputs the union mesh in the specified format. It must handle any scale efficiently and be correct.

Implementing a full CSG union from scratch is extremely complex. It involves:

1. Representing meshes as 3D geometry (triangles).

2. Detecting intersections between triangles of A and B.

3. Computing intersection points and clipping polygons.

4. Rebuilding the union surface without holes or self-intersections.

5. Ensuring manifold, watertight output.
