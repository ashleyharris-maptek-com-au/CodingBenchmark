**Weighing exact vs. approximate min cut methods**

The requirement to always output the exact minimum cut conflicts with scalability to massive graphs possibly exceeding memory. Stoer-Wagner suits small graphs; for larger ones, fallback to Karger's randomized contractions with many repetitions can balance correctness and scale, though it risks approximation. I'll plan an adaptive approach combining exact and probabilistic algorithms with careful trade-offs.**Designing adaptive exact and randomized min cut implementations**

Decided to implement Stoer-Wagner exact algorithm with adjacency lists and contraction using unordered_maps for graphs up to moderate size (2000–5000 vertices), tracking contracted vertex groups to output the cut partition and edges. For larger graphs, I'll implement Karger(-Stein) randomized contractions with repetitions and DSU-based tracking to produce a high-probability min cut partition, balancing correctness and scalability.
