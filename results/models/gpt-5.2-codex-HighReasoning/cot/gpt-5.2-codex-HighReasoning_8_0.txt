**Assessing BFS feasibility on huge maze**

Considering memory, storing visited, parent, and walls as bitsets for a 50k x 50k maze is borderline but manageable within 32GB RAM; however, BFS on 2.5 billion nodes is likely too slow in practice despite memory optimization.**Finalizing memory-efficient BFS with bitsets**

The plan refines BFS using bitsets for walls and u8 parent arrays to fit large mazes in memory, reading input line-by-line to avoid full storage, tracking start/end, and reconstructing the path by storing parent directions; though memory-heavy, this approach fits within 32GB and handles large inputs practically.
