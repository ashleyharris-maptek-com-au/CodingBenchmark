**Designing SPIR-V shader with stripes and Lambert shading**

I'm working through a detailed plan to create a SPIR-V fragment shader that combines Lambertian lighting with a striped color pattern based on sine of the UV coordinate. The plan covers normalizing the input normal and light direction vectors, computing the diffuse lighting term, setting up a uniform buffer for light position, generating alternating red and blue stripes via sine, and composing the final color with alpha. I'm carefully mapping out every SPIR-V instruction, type, constant, and decoration needed to produce a precise, efficient shader implementation. This includes handling vector operations like vector shuffle to extract xyz from vec4 and building the output vec4 color by combining the scaled stripe color with full alpha. It's a thorough and intricate approach to craft this shader fully in SPIR-V assembly text.
