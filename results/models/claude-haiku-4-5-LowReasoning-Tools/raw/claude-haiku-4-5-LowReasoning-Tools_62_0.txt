I've written a complete TypeScript solution for the wildfire firebreak problem. Here's my approach:

**Algorithm:**
1. **Fire Simulation** - BFS that spreads fire through burnable forest (`.`) while respecting water/rock barriers (`~`) and firebreaks
2. **Greedy Optimization** - Iteratively places firebreaks that save the most town value:
   - Simulate current fire state
   - Test each candidate cell to find which saves the most value if cut
   - Choose the best candidate and repeat until budget exhausted

**Efficiency optimizations:**
- Candidate cells are selected from areas adjacent to burning/burned zones (not entire grid)
- Early termination when no beneficial cuts remain
- Set-based tracking for O(1) lookups

The solution reads JSON from stdin, applies the greedy firebreak placement strategy, and outputs the result in the required format. It should scale well on the benchmark scenarios.
