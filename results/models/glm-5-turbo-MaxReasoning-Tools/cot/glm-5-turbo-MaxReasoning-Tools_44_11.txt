1. Renders a 6x6 UV polka-dot pattern with circular dots of radius 0.3 in cell space
2. Dots are yellow (1.0, 0.8, 0.0) on dark blue background (0.1, 0.1, 0.3)
3. Applies Lambertian shading with factor (0.3 + 0.7*diffuse)
4. Outputs alpha 1.0

For the polka-dot pattern:
- Take UV coordinates, scale by 6.0 to get cell coordinates
- Use fract to get local coordinates within each cell (centered at 0.5, 0.5)
- Compute distance from center, if < 0.3, it's a dot (yellow), otherwise background (dark blue)

For Lambertian shading:
- N = normalize(normal)
- L = normalize(lightPos.xyz - worldPos)
- diffuse = max(dot(N, L), 0.0)
- factor = 0.3 + 0.7 * diffuse

Then multiply the pattern color by the shading factor.
